Casting involves pouring molten aluminum into molds to create wheel shapes, offering versatility in design. Forging, on the other hand, involves shaping aluminum through a process of deformation, resulting in stronger and lighter wheels. Other methods may include extrusion or machining, which provide alternative production techniques. Each type has its advantages, influencing factors like weight, strength, cost, and aesthetic appeal in vehicle design.</p></p>
