<p><p>Stretch socks encompass various types designed for specific purposes, with gradient socks and anti-embolism socks being prominent categories. Gradient socks apply graduated compression, promoting blood circulation and reducing swelling, making them ideal for athletes and individuals with circulatory issues. In contrast, anti-embolism socks are designed for patients at risk of deep vein thrombosis, offering consistent compression to prevent blood clots during prolonged immobility, such as post-surgery or long-haul travel. Both types prioritize comfort and support for enhanced wellbeing.</p></p>
