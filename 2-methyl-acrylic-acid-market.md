<p><p>The 2-Methyl-Acrylic Acid market is primarily categorized into two methods of production: the Acetone Cyanohydrin Method and the Isobutylene Oxidation Method. The Acetone Cyanohydrin Method involves the reaction of acetone with hydrogen cyanide, yielding a high purity product suitable for various applications. In contrast, the Isobutylene Oxidation Method utilizes isobutylene as a feedstock, undergoing oxidation to produce 2-Methyl-Acrylic Acid. Each method offers distinct advantages in terms of cost, efficiency, and scalability, catering to different market demands.</p></p>
