<p><p>The cervical cancer diagnostic market encompasses several key types. Pap testing involves collecting cells from the cervix to identify abnormalities. HPV testing detects the presence of high-risk human papillomavirus strains that can lead to cervical cancer. Colposcopy offers a detailed examination of the cervix using a magnifying instrument, often following abnormal Pap results. Cervical biopsies involve the removal of tissue samples for pathological analysis to confirm cancer. Cystoscopy may be used to assess any urinary tract involvement in advanced cases.</p></p>
